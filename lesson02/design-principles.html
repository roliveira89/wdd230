Hick's Law

Hick's Law suggests that the time it takes to make a decision increases with the number of choices available.

Example: Airbnb

Visit Airbnb

Mobile View Screenshot

Airbnb is a popular online marketplace for lodging, primarily homestays for vacation rentals.

In the mobile view of Airbnb's website, Hick's Law is exemplified by the streamlined and intuitive booking process. The initial focus on a single search bar minimizes distractions and choices, guiding users to input their destination. As users proceed, options are presented step by step, gradually revealing more choices like check-in/out dates and the number of guests. This gradual presentation of choices reduces cognitive load, making the decision-making process manageable and aligning with Hick's Law. Airbnb optimizes the user experience and facilitates quicker decision-making through this approach.

PARC: Proximity

PARC: Proximity is a design principle that suggests related elements should be placed close to each other, while unrelated elements should have more space between them.

Example: Amazon Product Listing

Visit Amazon

Amazon organizes product details, including the product title, price, and product options (e.g., size, color), in close proximity. By grouping these elements together, Amazon provides users with a clear and organized presentation of essential product information. Shoppers can quickly access details they need to make informed purchase decisions without unnecessary distractions.

PARC: Alignment

PARC: Alignment involves organizing elements along a common axis to create a sense of order and structure.

Example: Apple's Product Page

Visit Apple

Apple's product page showcases aligned product images, titles, and descriptions in a clean and organized manner, creating a visually pleasing and user-friendly browsing experience.
